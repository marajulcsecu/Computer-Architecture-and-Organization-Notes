A program has a total running time of 100 ms and consists of three parts: A, B, and C. Part A accounts for 10% of the execution time (10 ms) and is improved by a factor of 2. Part B accounts for 30% of the execution time (30 ms) and is also improved by a factor of 2. Part C takes the remaining 60 ms and is not improved. Using Amdahl’s law, calculate the overall speedup of the program.
